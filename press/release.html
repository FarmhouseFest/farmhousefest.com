Farmhouse Fest Press Release

Feb 27th, 2018

Canada’s premier saison & wild ale festival returns to Vancouver July 7th, 2018.

Farmhouse Fest is a celebration of saisons, wild and sour ales that returns for its third year to the scenic UBC Farm on Saturday, July 7th, 2018.

Unique amongst beer festivals, Farmhouse Fest believes that good beer can be as complex and rewarding as the best Bordeaux or Islay malt. We focus exclusively on a narrow range of beer styles and meticulously curate a high-quality selection of limited experimental beer from select local and international breweries.

More than just excellent beer, it’s about being in an interesting place with great people. It’s about stretching out on a sun-drenched field and sipping a beer made with passion and craft. It’s about being as excited for great farmhouse ales as the brewers are.

The full story

Featuring over 50 hand-selected breweries from around the world, Farmhouse Fest is an international festival right here in Vancouver.

We obsessively focus on a narrow range of beer styles: Saisons, Wild Ales, and Sours. Our goal is quality over quantity, and as such we intentionally invite only those breweries who are quality-driven, experimental, and have a track record of producing great farmhouse beers.

The food selection is also curated, with some of Vancouver’s best food vendors on-site serving up a wide variety of unique international cuisine. Past years have included saltimbocca, west coast tacos, sous vide comfort food, modern Filipino, and high quality sandwiches.

The field playing host to Farmhouse Festival is situated on a 24 hectare model farm located on UBC’s main Point Grey campus. Picture a spacious grassy area surrounded by evergreens within arms length of a sustainable working farm.

By capping our tickets at comfortable numbers, we intentionally avoid the elbow-to-elbow crush of an oversold event. There’s plenty of room to spread out a blanket on the grass and within the small on-site apple orchard, or toss some beanbags in between the rustic barrels and hay bales dotted around the field.
